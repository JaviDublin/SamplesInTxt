Para gestionar estas fases, Euro Automotive Fabrication utiliza dos herramientas diferentes: una para planificar la cadena de produccion (Supply Chain Planning - SCP) y otra para ejecutar el plan (Supply Chain Execution - SCE). La planificacion se compone de varias funciones principales (Motiwalla & Thompson 2013): la planificacion de la adquisicion de materiales y servicios a los proveedores, la planificacion de la distribucion de los productos fabricados a los clientes en funcion de la demanda, la planificacion y la programacion de la produccion de los componentes,

La suma de la planificacion y de la ejecucion es igual a la gestion de la cadena de produccion que pertenece al modulo Supply Chain Management. El modulo SCM agrupa la planificacion y la ejecucion del plan en seis procesos diferentes (Motiwalla & Thompson 2011):
1 - Procurement – la gestion del las compras y ventas de bienes y servicios a otras empresas (B2B)

4 - Order Fulfillment – gestion de los procesos de pedido, almacenamiento y entreg de los componentes
5 - Customer Service Management – Base de datos con informacion del cliente en tiempo real sobre le demanda del producto, fechas de entrega y operaciones de produccion y distribucion
6 - Forecasting – prediccion de la actividad del producto en distintos rangos temporales.

La cuarta area del ciclo de vida de un cliente es retener al cliente (Retention) y por ultimo fidelizar al cliente (Loyalty). Todas las herramientas de un CRM estan diseñadas para contribuir en alguna de las areas del ciclo de vida, por ejemplo, la herramienta de investigacion de mercados para diseñar campañas de marketing que sirvan para atraer clientes nuevos o la herramienta de servicio al cliente sirve para intentar retener a los clientes cuando algo falla en el proceso.

Los sistemas de Empresa son productos disñados para gestionar todos los aspectos de cualquier negocio desde una unica plataforma. La necesidad de este tipo de plataformas surge porque las empresas, normalmente, crecen de forma organica buscando soluciones a corto plazo a los problemas y añadiendo a la estructura existente los nuevos productos, los nuevos departamentos y los nuevos procesos y el resultado es la aparicion de un gran numero de aplicaciones y bases de datos, de informacion duplicada y falta de automatizacion entre otros problemas.
Cuando una empresa como Euro Automotive Fabrication (EAF) decide implementar un Sisteme de Empresas, es muy dificil volver hacia atras. La decision debe ser pensada y madurada y el producto que se va a utilizar debe ser elegido con cuidado. Es esencial que la cultura de la empresa se adapte a la existencia del nuevo sistema porque la implementacion de un Sistema de empresas no es solamente la implementacion de unas herramientas de software, todos los procesos y tareas de la empresa van a girar entorno a este Sistema. Los sistemas de empresas tiene beneficios importantes como la reduccion de costes, la simplificacion de procesos o la estandarizacion de la informacion pero tambien exigen un compromiso de la empresa para mantener y actualizar el sistema, formar a los empleados e integrar el sistema con todas las demas herramientas de la organizacion. La estrategia a seguir para implementar un Sistema de Empresas depende del grado de madurez de la arquitectura de la organizacion. En Euro Automotive Fabrication (EAF) el sistema de compras esta fallando y el ERP se ha quedado obsoleto. En este caso es facil deducir que no se ha mantenido el Sistema de forma adecuada. La expansion del negocio a tres nuevos paises proporciona a la organizacion una oportunidad de revisar el sistema existente, actualizarlo y revisar los procesos para adaptar el negocio a los nuevos tiempos y no cometer errores evitables en los nuevos paises.

The processes and capabilities provided by Customer Relationship Management (CRM).

A crucial part of business is getting to know your customers and your customers ‘needs, in order to achieve this much care needs to be taken to ensure that you can deliver the customers’ needs and grow your customer base, this is the area that a customer relationship management CRM can help and in most organisations is now critical.  

According to (Wang, Feng, 2012) The CRM technology is used for better management of customers relationship and generally includes front and back office technology’s which supports sales and services and includes a data storage that can be integrated throughout different parts of the organisation so an analysis can be carried out about customers and strengthen customer related capability’s and provide greater profitability to the organisation.

The CRM also has its roots in relationship marketing which is aimed at improving long-run proﬁtability by shifting from transaction-based marketing, with its emphasis on winning new customers, to customer retention through effective management of customer relationships (Chen, Popovich, 2003). This often means trying to analyse customers behaviour which is known as Predictive analytics which essentially means correctly forecasting the future, and taking action to profit from those forecasts. It is a shift in business intelligence from information that is backward looking to forward-looking. (crmsearch.com)

The CRM can in business take three forms according to (Buttle, Maklan, 2015) which are listed below.

Strategic – the strategic CRM is focused on customer-centric business strategy, which is aimed at winning and retaining profitable customers through enhanced customer value which allows the company to collect customer information to allow and empower employees deliver better customer service to the customer.

Operational - the operational CRM has the ability to automate the marketing, selling and service function of the business, automation involves the application of technology to help and manage their service operations which can be done through call centres, field service or face to face with a high degree of efficiency, reliability, and effectiveness.

Analytical – the analytical CRM is concerned with capturing, storing, extracting and integrating customer-related data to enhance both customer and company value, and this can be done through real-time marketing where predictive modeling enables company’s make relevant offers to customers as they interact with the company at different touchpoints. At these touchpoints customer, related data can be anything from purchase history, credit and payment history to campaign response loyalty on their products or services.


CRM applications have now become quite powerful, their Web-enabled is designed to extend to the data mining capabilities of ERP throughout the supply chain to customers, distributors, and manufacturers and can share the results across channels within the organisations. However, there is a need to understand where the business process is and where it needs to go before plunging into CRM applications (Chen, Popovich, 2003). Once the case is made for CRM the organisation must identify and plan the realisation of CRM benefits, CRM should not be planned and managed as an IT project. Instead, management should focus on the benefits of CRM and not the technology itself.

Enterprise systems (ES) can be described as a combination of computer hardware and software utilised for organising and running operations for business purposes. The enterprise applications are developed to integrate the computer systems that run all phases of the business operations (e.g. sales, accounting, finance, human resources, and manufacturing). Ideally, businesses want to control all major ES operations in real time via a single system landscape (Enterprise Applications - Gartner IT Glossary).

Business Intelligence (BI) is an umbrella term for the applications, infrastructure and tools and best practices that enable access to and analyse of business information to improve and optimise decisions and performance (Business Intelligence - BI - Gartner IT Glossary). A typical enterprise system architecture for BI is shown below in Figure 1 (Chaudhuri, Dayal and Narasayya 2011). The data utilised in the BI operations is generated from multiple sources. With the cost of data procurement and data storage has declined significantly in recent years, businesses are starting to acquire very large data volumes to gain competitive advantage. With ES parallel architecture and analytics, businesses are pushing towards using data warehousing. The BI backend processes for preparing data is known as the Extract-Transform-Load (ETL) tools. The complex event processing (CEP) engines have been created to support the BI tasks in near real-time operation. The data from BI performed processes is typically placed into a repository called the data warehouse and is monitored by one or more warehouse servers.

Data Warehouse (DW) is a large collection store of generated data from a vast variety of sources from within the company and is utilised in enterprise-wide managerial decisions (Data warehouse | Definition of data warehouse in English by Oxford Dictionaries). A popular DW engine choice would be the relational database management system (RDBMS). Over the years, further development of data structures, optimisations and query processing techniques have been executed to handle complex SQL queries over large volumes of data. With digital data, there is an increase in demand for the architecture to be low-cost and businesses are facing the ‘Big Data’ Challenge. Driven by this challenge, engines are based on the MapReduce algorithm. This model is utilised for processing data using a parallel programming methodology (MapReduce - Gartner IT Glossary).

DW servers are accompanied by a set of mid-tier servers that provide specialised functionality for BI processes. The mid-tier servers include online analytic processing (OLAP), ES Engine, Reporting Server, and Data Mining. OLAP is a multi-dimensional technology for data discovery and provides the capability for complex calculations and data modelling (What is the Definition of OLAP? OLAP Definition).

To conclude, the umbrella term BI is utilised in ES to describe how the applications and infrastructure/tools enable access and analysis of business information which is stored in the data warehousing repository. Data acquisitions are becoming easier with the further developments of ES and large volumes of relational data (10s to 100s of terabytes) being stored by businesses are common. A typical business intelligence architecture will include data warehouse servers alongside mid-tier servers.
